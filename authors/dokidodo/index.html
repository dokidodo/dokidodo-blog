May 30, 2022

Today and This Month

I'm having a lot of trouble remembering the readings for "today" (今日) and "this month" (今月). For 今日, the reading is きょう (kyou) and is a complete exception. There is nothing to do but memorize it. In the case of 今月, the reading is こんげつ (kongetsu), which is at least a standard on'yomi reading for both kanji. You'd think that would make 今月 easier to remember. However, I think the visual similarity it shares with 今日 is causing me to trip over it as if 今月 also had an exceptional reading. In other news, I've joined tokiniandy.com to assist with studying the Genki textbooks. It seems useful so far, but I haven't gone through too many of the lessons yet. We'll see how it goes. If nothing else, I appreciate that both WaniKani and ToKini Andy seem to have very active and helpful communities.

WaniKani Level 4

I crossed into level four on WaniKani today. According to my stats, I've learned 80 radicals, 80 kanji, and 124 vocabulary words. I started with WaniKani 32 days ago. It's certainly not the fastest progression I've seen, but I'm very happy with where I'm at. Full stats: I'm also pretty satisfied with my accuracy. Remembering the meaning of the radicals/kanji/vocab has (so far) been fairly easy. I've had to focus much harder on the readings. Counting things/people/days/months/etc. tripped me up quite a bit. Knowing when to use which reading of a number has been a bit of a struggle, but I finally feel like I'm getting it right almost every time. There is a local chapter of the National Association fo Japan-America Societies in my city that offers Japanese language classes. They are (understandably) not inexpensive, but I may consider trying one at some point, if only for the conversation and listening practice I would gain.
